Introduction and Background

The music industry has a unique relationship with gender, and gender representation in the industry. In the US music industry, the number of men outnumbers the number of women, especially in roles of power such as songwriters and producers. According to a recent 2021 USC diversity report in the music industry, about 21 percent of artists are women, about 12 percent are songwriters and about 2 percent are producers.

The imbalance in gender and power in music influences all aspects of the industry, down to the songs that are performed by artists and written for artists. Gender and love had played an important role in music and for many to express themselves, especially after a breakup or splitting from their partner. Many song lyrics have gendered themes that are often based on gender stereotypes and focused on gendered roles, leading to music having gendered language.

The data used for this piece comes from scraping data from the Billboard Top 100 charts, Billboard End of the Year Charts, and various lyric sites. The songs that were picked to create the corpus and to analyze all share the same theme of a breakup, to count as a breakup song the lyrics had to explicitly talk about an ex or heavily imply the song is about an ex romantic relationship. To analyze the data and lyrics I had used a mixture of manual data analysis and using tools such as R, AntConc, and Vovant Tools.

Analysis Music Themes and Lyrics

Many of the songs that were discussing love and breakups follow similar themes and contents in the lyrics. Overall the song's themes, regardless of gender, focus on the major themes of anger and emotions as well as negative feelings towards their exes.

Regardless of the gender identity of the singer, a similarity between male and female singers is that the structures of the songs tend to focus on three main aspects of the breakup and relationship. The first aspect is the singers themselves, the second is what the ex, and lastly the relationship.

Lines of themselves include the singer's feelings or actions that they have done or felt because of the breakup. While lines about their exes focus on what the ex has done since the breakup or how they might be feeling. Lastly, the lines about the effects of the relationship usually focus on things on how the relationship made the singer feel or that it made a worse or better person.

However, even with similar structures in music the lyrics themselves are usually heavily gendered. Many pop songs' lyrics fall victim to troops and gendered stereotypes, especially when talking about love and exes.

The lyrics in the male artist songs tend to be more anger-focused and often follow an action due to the relationship.

The lyrics in the female artist's songs tend to be more focused on themselves and how they're feeling. As well as many songs focus on the artist thinking or watching the impacts of the relationship and break up.

Songs with neutral lyrics tend to be less explicit with the major themes and were in the middle of the spectrum of breakup songs.

Pronouns & Themes

Male singers tend to focus on what they are going to do after the breakup, like go party, go drink or do drugs, or even have sex or sleep around. While they tend to be angry towards their ex and call them names and blame them for the breakup.

Female artists tend to focus mostly on their emotions, and thinking about the relationship, and then focusing on themselves after the event.

However, there are still overlapping themes of emotions caused by the breakup and what happened afterward. As well not all songs were as explicit with these themes and there were songs that were in the middle of the spectrum. The main themes were things like anger and regret and the aftermaths of the breakup, the main difference was what language was used and how the artists talk about the breakup or their ex. For example, how artists would describe and talk about their ex and how they felt because of the breakup, as well as how they described their actions after the breakup. (Did they party after the breakup or did they talk about it with friends?)

Gender in Music

Gender in music has been a topic that has been an issue throughout time, with more and more artists speaking out on the inequalities they faced in the industry. This issue creates an unhealthy culture for those in the industry and allows for sexism to thrive. There are more men in the music industry compared to women, leading to an imbalance of representation in the industry.

The lack of representation in the industry creates the issue of how songs are written and what goes on behind the scenes. The imbalance of gender leads to songs having gendered themes and stereotypes that influence the meaning of the song.

Regardless of the artist's gender identity, the majority of their songwriters and producers are men, often many of these teams are made of all men or a small handful of women. However, even with a majority of the songwriters and producers being males, there are differences in how songs are written depending on the artist that was singing the song.

Methodology

To analyze gender pronouns and themes in the songs, lyric data was collected online and then entered into AntConc and Voyent Tools, allowing for lyrical analysis such as word combinations and collecting usage of pronouns. While R was used to create the data visualizations of the pronouns, I used Julia Silge's R code Gender Roles with Text Mining and N-grams to help create visualizations. In addition, to the R code Flourish was used to make some other graphics.

Artists', songwriters', and producers' gender identities were collected from their Wikipedia page and online articles and news sources. Song data come from lyric sites such as Genius and AZLyrics, and songs that were chosen came from the Billboard 100's charts. The songs that were picked had lyrics and themes that had some sort of references to a past relationship or an ex.
